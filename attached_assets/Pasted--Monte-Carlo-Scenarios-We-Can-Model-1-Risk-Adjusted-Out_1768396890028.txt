  Monte Carlo Scenarios We Can Model

  1. Risk-Adjusted Outcome Projections

  "What is the probability distribution of outcomes for a specific patient cohort?"

  Examples:
  - "What's the 90% confidence interval for 5-year revision rate in patients with osteoporosis?"
  - "Simulate 10,000 iterations for patients age >70 - what's the expected survival distribution?"

  How it works: Sample HRs from their CIs, apply to baseline registry rates, aggregate N iterations.

  ---
  2. Enrollment Criteria Impact Analysis

  "How would different enrollment criteria affect expected study outcomes?"

  Examples:
  - "If we exclude BMI>35 patients, what's the probability of achieving <10% revision rate at 2 years?"
  - "Compare enrollment with vs without age cap at 80 - show probability distributions"

  How it works: Filter cohort by criteria, run Monte Carlo on filtered vs unfiltered populations.

  ---
  3. Protocol Success Probability

  "What's the probability our study will meet its primary endpoint?"

  Examples:
  - "Given our current cohort risk profile, what's the probability of achieving the protocol target (>50% MCID achievement)?"
  - "Simulate: what % of iterations show 2-year survival >90%?"

  How it works: Run N iterations, count how many meet the success threshold → P(success).

  ---
  4. Sensitivity Analysis on Risk Factors

  "Which risk factors have the most impact on outcome uncertainty?"

  Examples:
  - "Run Monte Carlo with/without osteoporosis HR uncertainty - how much narrower is the CI?"
  - "Which risk factor contributes most to outcome variance?"

  How it works: Systematically vary one HR at a time while holding others constant.

  ---
  5. Multi-Factor Interaction Effects

  "How do combined risk factors affect outcomes probabilistically?"

  Examples:
  - "Patient with osteoporosis AND age>80 AND BMI>35 - what's their 5-year revision distribution?"
  - "Compare: single high-risk factor vs multiple moderate-risk factors"

  How it works: Multiply sampled HRs together, show how uncertainty compounds.

  ---
  6. Time-to-Event Projections

  "When are outcomes most uncertain?"

  Examples:
  - "Show uncertainty bands at 1yr, 2yr, 5yr, 10yr for high-risk cohort"
  - "At what time point does the confidence interval exceed ±5%?"

  How it works: Use registry survival curves at multiple time points, propagate uncertainty.

  ---
  7. Comparative Effectiveness Scenarios

  "Compare outcome distributions across different patient subgroups"

  Examples:
  - "Monte Carlo for females vs males - show overlapping distribution histograms"
  - "Compare revision risk distributions: Paprosky 2B vs 3B defects"

  How it works: Run separate simulations, output side-by-side distributions.
