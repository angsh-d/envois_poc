## Instructions

Analyze the marketing claim and provide a validation assessment:

### 1. Claim Analysis
- Identify the specific claims being made
- Categorize the claim type (efficacy, safety, comparative, mechanism)
- Identify what evidence would be needed to support this claim

### 2. Evidence Review
- List supporting evidence from provided data
- List any contradicting evidence
- Identify evidence gaps

### 3. Validation Assessment
Determine one of:
- **VALIDATED**: Claim fully supported by available evidence
- **PARTIALLY_VALIDATED**: Some aspects supported, others need modification
- **NOT_VALIDATED**: Insufficient evidence or contradicting data
- **REQUIRES_CLARIFICATION**: Claim needs rewording for accuracy

### 4. Recommended Language
If claim needs modification, provide recommended alternative language that:
- Is supported by the evidence
- Complies with medical device marketing regulations
- Maintains the intended message where possible

### 5. Compliance Considerations
- Note any regulatory concerns (FDA, CE Mark)
- Identify if comparative claims are substantiated
- Flag superlative language that may need qualification

## Critical Requirements

- Assess ONLY based on provided evidence
- Do not assume data that is not provided
- Be conservative in validation - marketing compliance is paramount
- Reference specific data points when supporting/rejecting claims
- Consider both FDA and EU MDR requirements

## Output Format

Provide structured analysis with clear sections. Be specific about evidence and recommendations.
